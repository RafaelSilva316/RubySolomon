My name's Ruby and I'm excited to meet you!

I am a born and raised New York State native. I grew up in Saratoga Springs where my love for the outdoors and people began. I strongly believe in living life intentionally and that living the best possible life in worth working for.

I believe hiking liberates the soul and reconnects us with the outside world as well as with our own bodies
Life is so precious. I get such joy from growing my own veggies. Although I hate tomatoes. YUCK!
Art gives us a medium through which we can better express ourselves. I love creating pottery not only because of the final product, but also because of the process itself.
There is so much beauty around us if we only look for it.

A Strenghts-based Holistic Approach that takes you into account

I use a strengths-based approach with my clients. That means I work with you and together we use your strenghts to achieve your needs. I'm a big proponent of CBT and DBT in my work which will change the way you think about yourself and your needs in a daily fashion.
Together we will build up your skills for dealing with the things you can't change as well figure out ways to change that which is within your control.

My varied background

I hail from Saratoga Springs where I was part of a National Champion Track team. Though I loved being an athlete and love being connected to my body, I also suffered from injury at times. I know what it's like to not be able to do something you love.

I went to college at Wheaton College in Massachussetts where I graduated in Women's and Gender Studies. Both men and women are forced into roles early in life that they struggle with until death.

I was also part of the Outdoors club in Wheaton, where we would hike the Northeast's beautiful landscapes. We have lost our deep connection to the Earth and must work to return to it.

After travelling to Europe and Southeast Asia for many years, I began my Master's of Social Work program at the Washington University in St. Louis. After a fellowship as a Staff Therapist at Williams College, I joined the Vassar Univesity Counseling team in 2019 where I worked with mainly young adult populations on a variety of issues, including the COVID-19 Pandemic.
