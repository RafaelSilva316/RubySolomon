Ruby specializes in you

Ruby's primary areas of care include sexual identity and functioning, gender identity, relationship structures, grief work, existential concerns, sports psychology, identity exploration and social justice perspectives.

Approach

Ruby’s therapeutic approach is integrative, collaborative, and strengths-based. She draws from a variety of therapeutic modalities depending on a student’s particular needs and preferences and holds in mind a student’s existing creativity, intelligence, kindness, power, resilience, etc. throughout the healing process.

Education

She received her Bachelor of Arts in Women’s and Gender Studies from Wheaton College in Massachusetts and her Master’s in Social Work from Washington University in St. Louis. Ruby completed a postgraduate fellowship emphasizing holistic and multicultural therapeutic care in the counseling center at Williams College and has specialized training in the field of sexuality education.

Personal

In her spare time, Ruby enjoys traveling, exercising, reading, crafting art, watching stand-up comedy, and listening to spoken word poetry.
